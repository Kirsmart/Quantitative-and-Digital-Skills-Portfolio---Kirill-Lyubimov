This project about comparison of Weekly stock data for Microsoft (MSFT), Apple (AAPL), and the S&P 500. The analysis moves from descriptive statistics, also through hypothesis testing to regression, producing a data-driven investment assessment of both companies.
In this analysis I developed some proficiency in applying quantitative and statistical methods to real existing financial data. Working with weekly stock returns for Microsoft, Apple, and Alphabet, I practised computing descriptive statistics and used the IQR fencing method to identify return outliers. I conducted parametric hypothesis tests, applying both a Chi-Square test to evaluate variance against a benchmark and an F-test to compare the risk profiles of two stocks respectfully. Finally, I estimated a simple linear regression model to measure the relationship between two companies (Microsoft and Alphabet), interpreting R-squared, regression coefficients in an investment context.
